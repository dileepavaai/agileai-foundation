# Ecosystem Integrity Charter  
Agile AI Institutional Architecture  

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active  
Effective: March 2026  
Authority: Agile AI Foundation  

---

## 1. Purpose

This Charter establishes the structural integrity principles governing the Agile AI institutional ecosystem.

It exists to preserve:

- Canonical authority
- Governance discipline
- Layer separation
- Version stability
- Institutional credibility

The long-term legitimacy of Agile AI as a discipline depends on adherence to these principles.

---

## 2. Institutional Authority Hierarchy

The ecosystem operates under a fixed and non-negotiable authority flow:

1. **Foundation defines WHAT must exist.**
2. **University explains and applies.**
3. **Assessment evaluates alignment.**
4. **Credentials represent validated capability.**

Reverse authority is prohibited.

No runtime system, learning surface, assessment mechanism, or credential layer may redefine the canonical Functional Elements.

---

## 3. Canonical Protection Doctrine

The canonical surface (`agileai.foundation`) is:

- Non-commercial  
- Non-certifying  
- Framework-agnostic  
- Constitutionally governed  

Modifications require:

- Version increment  
- Changelog entry  
- Governance record  
- Archive preservation  

Silent modification is strictly prohibited.

Canonical stability takes precedence over expansion.

---

## 4. Layer Separation Principle

The ecosystem is intentionally layered to prevent authority collapse.

### Canon Layer  
Defines structural requirements.

### Learning Layer  
Interprets and contextualizes canonical structure.

### Assessment Layer  
Measures alignment against canonical requirements.

### Credential Layer  
Represents demonstrated capability, not attendance.

### Runtime Layer  
Implements operational systems supporting the above layers.

Layer merging or role confusion weakens institutional clarity and is not permitted.

---

## 5. Revenue Doctrine

The Foundation:

- Must not sell  
- Must not certify  
- Must not issue credentials  
- Must not embed marketing calls-to-action  

Revenue-generating activities belong to University and Executive Insight layers.

Commercial incentives must not influence canonical definitions.

---

## 6. Version Discipline

Version evolution is governed as follows:

- **v1.x** → Clarification only (backward compatible)
- **v2.0** → Structural revision requiring:
  - Public whitepaper
  - Archived prior version
  - Formal governance declaration

Stability over novelty.  
Governance over growth.

---

## 7. Institutional Drift Prevention

The following actions constitute structural drift:

- Altering canonical definitions without version increment  
- Inserting promotional language into canonical surfaces  
- Allowing runtime systems to redefine structural meaning  
- Allowing commercial pressure to influence definitions  
- Retroactively modifying governance records  

Institutional drift erodes authority and is prohibited.

---

## 8. Governance Before Scaling

Institutional growth must follow governance maturity.

No scaling initiative may:

- Blur canonical ownership  
- Collapse layer separation  
- Bypass version discipline  
- Prioritize expansion over structural integrity  

Credibility compounds slowly and can be weakened quickly.

---

## 9. Preservation Obligation

All institutional actors operating within the Agile AI ecosystem are obligated to:

- Preserve canonical clarity  
- Respect authority boundaries  
- Maintain transparent version traceability  
- Avoid semantic dilution  

Integrity is cumulative and must be actively protected.

---

## 10. Charter Status

This Charter is active as of March 2026.

Amendments require:

- Explicit revision record  
- Governance note  
- Transparent documentation  

This Charter serves as the structural safeguard of the Agile AI institutional ecosystem.
