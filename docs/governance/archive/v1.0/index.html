Agile Artificial Intelligence (Agile AI) is defined by a small set of Functional Elements. These elements describe what must exist for AI-enabled products, projects, and services to be adaptive, responsible, and outcome-driven.

The Agile AI Functional Elements are framework-agnostic, technology-neutral, and durable. They define essential capabilities — not practices, methods, tools, certifications, or assessments.

These elements are governed, versioned, and maintained as a canonical reference. Their evolution is intentionally rare and controlled.

The Six Agile AI Functional Elements

1. Intent & Outcome Alignment

Agile AI systems must be explicitly aligned to clear intent and desired outcomes.

2. Human Judgment & Accountability

Agile AI requires deliberate human judgment and clear accountability.

3. Adaptive Execution

Agile AI systems must continuously adapt based on feedback and learning.

4. Continuous Learning & Feedback

Agile AI systems must learn continuously from outcomes and signals.

5. Governance by Design

Governance is embedded intentionally into Agile AI systems.

6. Systemic Integration

Agile AI operates as part of a broader socio-technical system.
