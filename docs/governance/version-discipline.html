Agile AI Foundation — Governance Authority

Version Discipline Policy

This policy defines the formal version governance framework governing Agile AI Functional Elements and all associated institutional publications.

1. Purpose

The Agile AI Foundation maintains strict version discipline to preserve structural stability, archival integrity, and long-term canonical authority.

Versioning ensures that structural evolution is deliberate, transparent, and formally recorded.

2. Structural vs Non-Structural Change

Structural Change

A structural change modifies the canonical definition of Agile AI Functional Elements.

  • Addition or removal of Functional Elements
  • Modification of element definitions
  • Reclassification or structural reordering
  • Alteration of governance authority boundaries

Structural changes require major version advancement and formal archival of prior versions.

Non-Structural Change

A non-structural change preserves canonical structure while improving clarity or publication quality.

  • Artifact publication (PDF rendering)
  • Formatting adjustments
  • Clarifications without definitional change
  • Governance documentation updates
  • Integrity verification metadata (checksums, IDs)

3. Semantic Versioning Framework

The Foundation follows a three-tier semantic version structure:

  • Major (2.0, 3.0, …)
    Structural revision of Functional Elements.
  • Minor (1.1, 1.2, …)
    Non-structural clarification or institutional enhancement.
  • Patch (1.1.1, 1.1.2, …)
    Technical corrections, artifact updates, or metadata adjustments.

Major version advancement requires formal governance declaration and permanent archival of all prior major versions.

4. Archival Integrity

All published versions are immutable once released. Archived versions remain permanently accessible and are never modified retroactively.

Canonical authority resides in the official HTML publication. Artifact renderings (PDF) are derivative but formally recorded.

5. Permanent Reference Identifiers

The Foundation assigns immutable reference identifiers to canonical documents and official artifacts.

Identifier Format: 

AAF-FAE-1.0-2025
AAF-FAE-1.0-PDF-2026
AAF-RD-1.1.0-2026

Reference identifiers remain permanently bound to their original publication and never change.
