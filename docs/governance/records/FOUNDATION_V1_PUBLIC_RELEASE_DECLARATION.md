# Agile AI Foundation  
## Constitutional Public Release Declaration  
### Agile AI Functional Elements v1.0  

**Canonical Reference ID:** AAF-FAE-1.0-2025  
**Release Status:** Active · Canonical · Public  
**Effective Date:** 01 January 2025  

---

## 1. Declaration of Release

The Agile AI Foundation formally declares the public release of:

**Agile AI Functional Elements v1.0**

This version establishes the authoritative, canonical definition of the Six Functional Elements required for Agile AI systems.

Version 1.0 is hereby designated as the foundational reference state for the Agile AI domain under the governance authority of the Agile AI Foundation.

---

## 2. Constitutional Freeze

Effective upon this declaration:

- Version 1.0 is constitutionally frozen.
- The Six Functional Elements are structurally stable.
- No additions, removals, or redefinitions are permitted prior to a major version advancement (v2.0).
- Interpretive clarifications may occur, but canonical structure shall not change.

Structural evolution, if ever required, will occur only through deliberate major version governance action.

---

## 3. Governance Authority

Governance authority over the Agile AI Functional Elements resides exclusively with the Agile AI Foundation.

Learning resources, derivative interpretations, training materials, certifications, assessments, consulting offerings, or commercial implementations do not redefine, supersede, or alter the canonical elements.

Version control is centralized and formally managed.

---

## 4. Scope and Positioning

Agile AI Functional Elements v1.0 defines:

- What must exist within Agile AI systems  
- Foundational capabilities  
- Governance-aligned structural elements  

This release does not define:

- Prescriptive methodologies  
- Commercial frameworks  
- Certification standards  
- Training programs  
- Tool-specific implementations  

The model is framework-agnostic, technology-neutral, and long-term in orientation.
