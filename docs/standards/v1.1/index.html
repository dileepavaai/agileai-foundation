2. The Six Agile AI Functional Elements

2.1 Intent & Outcome Alignment

Agile AI systems must be explicitly aligned to clear intent and desired outcomes. AI capability exists to serve outcomes rather than isolated technical outputs.

2.2 Human Judgment & Accountability

Human authority remains responsible for direction, constraints, and ethical boundaries governing AI-enabled systems and their operation.

2.3 Adaptive Execution

Agile AI systems must continuously adapt based on feedback, environmental signals, and evolving operational context.

2.4 Continuous Learning & Feedback

Systems must learn continuously from outcomes, signals, and experience to improve future capability and performance.

2.5 Governance by Design

Governance mechanisms must be intentionally embedded within Agile AI systems, ensuring accountability, transparency, and responsible operation.

2.6 Systemic Integration

Agile AI operates within broader socio-technical systems. Effective implementation requires integration with organizational, human, and technological structures.
