AGILE AI FOUNDATION — REFERENCE IMPLEMENTATION

Reference Implementation Examples

This page provides illustrative examples showing how the Agile AI Functional Elements may be applied when designing and operating AI-enabled systems.

These examples are informational and do not constitute additional standards. They exist to help practitioners interpret and apply the canonical specification.

Agile AI Conceptual Model

Agile AI systems operate through the coordinated interaction of three core forces:

  • Agile Practices — enabling adaptive planning, rapid iteration, and continuous improvement.
  • Business Artificial Intelligence — applying AI capabilities to decision-making, automation, and insight generation.
  • Human Judgment — ensuring contextual interpretation, governance, and responsible oversight.

When these forces operate together, organizations can design and operate AI-enabled systems that deliver the right outcomes at the right time.

Illustrative System Structure

An AI-enabled system designed using the Agile AI Functional Elements may include the following structural components.

1. Outcome Definition

The system begins with a clearly defined desired outcome. Agile planning practices ensure that the outcome remains adaptable as new insights emerge.

2. AI Capability Integration

Artificial intelligence capabilities are integrated to support prediction, pattern recognition, automation, or decision support. These capabilities augment organizational processes rather than replacing human responsibility.

3. Adaptive Delivery Loop

Agile delivery cycles continuously refine the system through iterative learning, data feedback, and operational observation.

4. Human Oversight and Judgment

Human expertise interprets AI outputs, evaluates contextual risk, and ensures responsible use of automated recommendations.

5. Continuous Learning

Operational feedback improves both the AI models and the surrounding organizational processes. This enables systems to evolve as conditions change.

Example Scenario

Consider a healthcare operations platform using Agile AI principles.

  • Outcome: Improve patient scheduling efficiency.
  • AI Capability: Predict patient appointment demand using historical data.
  • Agile Practices: Iteratively refine scheduling algorithms through short delivery cycles.
  • Human Judgment: Medical administrators review AI recommendations to ensure clinical priorities are respected.
  • Continuous Learning: System performance data improves future scheduling predictions.

This example illustrates how the Agile AI Functional Elements may coordinate AI capabilities, human expertise, and Agile practices to improve operational outcomes.
