AGILE AI FOUNDATION — CONCEPT OVERVIEW

FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPT

What is Agile AI?

Agile AI is a structural reference model for the design, development, and operation of AI-enabled systems that deliver the right outcomes at the right time through the coordinated application of Agile practices, artificial intelligence capabilities, and human judgment supported by continuous capability development.

Agile AI is framework-neutral and can be applied alongside existing delivery and scaling approaches such as Scrum, Kanban, SAFe, XP, or hybrid enterprise models. The model focuses on improving outcome alignment, decision quality, and adaptive execution without requiring structural changes to existing delivery frameworks.

The Agile AI concept focuses on improving how organizations design, build, and operate AI-enabled systems so that they can adapt to changing conditions while maintaining reliability, transparency, and responsible decision-making.

The approach emphasizes the integration of three complementary forces:

Agile Practices

Artificial Intelligence Capabilities

Human Judgment

  • Agile practices
    Adaptive methods that enable iterative development, continuous improvement, and rapid response to changing conditions.
  • Artificial intelligence capabilities
    Data-driven systems and models that support prediction, automation, and intelligent decision support.
  • Human judgment
    Responsible oversight, contextual understanding, and ethical decision-making applied to AI-enabled systems.

Purpose of Agile AI

Agile AI provides a structured approach for improving how AI-enabled systems are designed, deployed, and operated within organizations. The approach aims to ensure that AI systems remain adaptive, accountable, and aligned with evolving operational needs.

Rather than focusing only on model development, Agile AI addresses the broader system context in which artificial intelligence capabilities operate, including governance, operational feedback, and human decision-making.

Relationship to the Agile AI Standard

The structural elements required for Agile AI systems are formally defined in the Agile AI Functional Elements Standard published by the Agile AI Foundation.

The standard establishes the canonical structural elements that enable organizations to design and operate Agile AI systems in a consistent and interpretable way.

Institutional Role of the Foundation

The Agile AI Foundation operates as an independent academic and professional body that defines structured frameworks and canonical standards for the Agile AI domain.

The Foundation maintains the Agile AI standards through formal governance, version discipline, and archival preservation of previous releases.
