The Six Agile AI Functional Elements

1. Intent & Outcome Alignment

Agile AI systems must be explicitly aligned to clear intent and desired outcomes. AI capability exists to serve outcomes, not isolated outputs.

2. Human Judgment & Accountability

Agile AI requires deliberate human judgment and clear accountability. Humans remain responsible for direction, constraints, and ethical decisions, even as automation and autonomy increase.

3. Adaptive Execution

Agile AI systems must continuously adapt based on feedback, learning, and emerging signals. Change is treated as a constant condition.

4. Continuous Learning & Feedback

Agile AI systems must learn continuously from outcomes, signals, and impacts across individual, team, system, and system-of-systems levels.

5. Governance by Design

Governance is embedded intentionally into Agile AI systems. Ethical boundaries, constraints, and escalation paths are designed upfront and evolve with capability.

6. Systemic Integration

Agile AI operates as part of a broader socio-technical system. Interdependencies and second-order effects are considered to avoid fragmentation and local optimization.
