This guide provides structural interpretation only. Authoritative structural definitions reside exclusively within Agile AI Functional Elements v1.0 (Archive Snapshot).

Agile Artificial Intelligence (Agile AI) is defined by six co-foundational Functional Elements. These elements establish the structural conditions required for adaptive, responsible, and outcome-aligned AI systems.

Structural Overview

Agile AI v1.0 is structural rather than procedural, systemic rather than sequential, and framework-independent rather than tool-bound.

The six Functional Elements operate in systemic interrelation. Structural integrity emerges from coherence across all elements — not from procedural ordering.

  • Intent & Outcome Alignment
  • Human Judgment & Accountability
  • Adaptive Execution
  • Continuous Learning & Feedback
  • Governance by Design
  • Systemic Integration

Systemic Logic

The Functional Elements exist in defined interrelation. They cannot be adopted partially without structural distortion.

Selective implementation constitutes fragmentation and invalidates canonical alignment.

Interpretation may evolve; structural definition may not.
