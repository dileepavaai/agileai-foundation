2. Authority & Scope Declaration

This Guide is issued by the Agile AI Foundation as the official interpretive reference to Agile AI Functional Elements v1.0 (Canonical Reference ID: AAF-FAE-1.0-2025).

Agile AI Functional Elements v1.0 remains the sole authoritative structural definition of Agile AI. The English-language edition is definitive.

This Guide clarifies structural interpretation and defines the boundaries within which the canonical standard is to be understood and applied.

  • Does not alter or expand the Functional Elements
  • Does not introduce additional structural components
  • Does not establish training methodology
  • Does not define certification standards
  • Does not prescribe tools, vendors, or implementation frameworks

Where ambiguity exists, the canonical standard prevails.

3. Structural Overview of Agile AI v1.0

Agile AI v1.0 is defined through six co-foundational Functional Elements that collectively establish its structural system.

  • Intent & Outcome Alignment
  • Human Judgment & Accountability
  • Adaptive Execution
  • Continuous Learning & Feedback
  • Governance by Design
  • Systemic Integration

Each Functional Element represents a distinct but interdependent component. Structural integrity depends upon systemic coherence across all six.

Agile AI is structural rather than procedural, systemic rather than sequential, and framework-independent rather than tool-bound.

4. Systemic Logic

The Functional Elements exist in defined interrelation. They cannot be treated as independent modules.

Structural validity arises from systemic coherence — not procedural order.

Selective adoption or isolated implementation constitutes structural fragmentation and invalidates canonical alignment.

5. Interpretation Principles

  • Systemic Coherence — Elements must be interpreted in relation to the whole.
  • Non-Substitution — Elements cannot be replaced by tools or methodologies.
  • Context Neutrality — Structural definition remains independent of domain.
  • Non-Fragmentation — Partial adoption does not constitute alignment.
  • Evolution Without Structural Mutation — Interpretation may evolve; structure may not.

6. Boundary Conditions

Agile AI is not:

  • A tool stack or vendor framework
  • A transformation program
  • A maturity ladder
  • A certification pathway
  • A consulting methodology

7. Application Domains

Agile AI applies wherever AI systems influence enterprise operations, product ecosystems, or decision architectures.

Context affects application. It does not alter structural definition.

8. Governance & Version Control

Structural definitions are fixed within each major version. Amendments require formal version increment and archival preservation.

No silent modification is permitted.

9. Glossary

Canonical terms are defined exclusively within Agile AI Functional Elements v1.0.

10. Institutional Statement

Agile AI remains structurally defined, systemically coherent, and governance-controlled under the custodial authority of the Agile AI Foundation.
