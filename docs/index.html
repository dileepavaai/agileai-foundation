Agile Artificial Intelligence (Agile AI) is defined by a structured set of Functional Elements. These elements describe what must exist for AI-enabled systems, initiatives, and institutions to operate in an adaptive, accountable, and outcome-aligned manner.

The Functional Elements are framework-agnostic, technology-neutral, and durable. They define structural capability requirements — not tools, certifications, methodologies, or commercial programs.

Version 1.1 formalizes the framework under a signed constitutional governance baseline. Evolution is controlled, versioned, and publicly archived.

The Six Agile AI Functional Elements

1. Intent & Outcome Alignment

AI capability must be explicitly aligned to defined institutional intent and measurable outcomes. Outputs alone do not constitute alignment.

2. Human Judgment & Accountability

Human authority remains responsible for direction, constraint-setting, ethical boundaries, and final accountability.

3. Adaptive Execution

Agile AI systems operate under continuous adaptation informed by feedback, signal detection, and contextual change.

4. Continuous Learning & Feedback

Learning occurs across individual, team, system, and ecosystem layers, ensuring institutional evolution over static deployment.

5. Governance by Design

Governance structures are embedded intentionally into system design, including constraints, escalation paths, and boundary conditions.

6. Systemic Integration

Agile AI operates within broader socio-technical systems. Interdependencies and second-order impacts are explicitly considered.

Authority & Interpretation Boundaries

The Functional Elements define structural requirements for Agile AI. They do not constitute certification, compliance designation, or evaluative judgment.

Educational institutions, assessments, and applied programs may interpret or evaluate alignment to these elements; however, they do not redefine them.

  • Foundation — defines and governs the Functional Elements
  • Learning — supports structured understanding and application
  • Assessment — evaluates contextual alignment

About the Agile AI Foundation
