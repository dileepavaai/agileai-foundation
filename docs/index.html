Definition

Agile Artificial Intelligence (Agile AI) is defined by a small set of Functional Elements. These elements describe what must exist for AI-enabled products, systems, and services to be adaptive, responsible, and outcome-driven.

The Functional Elements are framework-agnostic, technology-neutral, and durable. They define essential capabilities — not practices, methods, tools, or certifications.

The Six Agile AI Functional Elements

1. Intent & Outcome Alignment

AI systems must be explicitly aligned to clear intent and desired outcomes. Capability exists to serve outcomes — not outputs.

2. Human Judgment & Accountability

Agile AI requires deliberate human judgment and clear accountability. Humans remain responsible for direction, constraints, and ethical decisions.

3. Adaptive Execution

Agile AI systems continuously adapt based on feedback, learning, and emerging signals. Change is a constant condition.

4. Continuous Learning & Feedback

Learning occurs across individual, team, system, and system-of-systems levels through outcomes, signals, and impacts.

5. Governance by Design

Governance is embedded intentionally into AI systems. Ethical boundaries, constraints, and escalation paths are designed upfront.

6. Systemic Integration

Agile AI operates within broader socio-technical systems. Interdependencies and second-order effects are explicitly considered.

Governance & Versioning

This page is the canonical reference for Agile AI Functional Elements v1.0. Practices, learning resources, assessments, and certifications may reference these elements but must not redefine them.
