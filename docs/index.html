Agile AI Functional Elements

Version 1.0 · Canonical Release

This document constitutes the authoritative and canonical definition of Agile AI Functional Elements. Governance authority resides exclusively with the Agile AI Foundation.

Light · Dark · System

Agile Artificial Intelligence (Agile AI) is defined by a small set of Functional Elements. These elements describe what must exist for AI-enabled products, projects, and services to be adaptive, responsible, and outcome-driven.

The Agile AI Functional Elements are framework-agnostic, technology-neutral, and durable. They define essential capabilities — not practices, methods, tools, certifications, or assessments.

These elements are governed, versioned, and maintained as a canonical reference. Their evolution is intentionally rare and controlled.

The Six Agile AI Functional Elements

1. Intent & Outcome Alignment

Agile AI systems must be explicitly aligned to clear intent and desired outcomes. AI capability exists to serve outcomes, not isolated outputs.

2. Human Judgment & Accountability

Agile AI requires deliberate human judgment and clear accountability. Humans remain responsible for direction, constraints, and ethical decisions, even as automation and autonomy increase.

3. Adaptive Execution

Agile AI systems must continuously adapt based on feedback, learning, and emerging signals. Change is treated as a constant condition.

4. Continuous Learning & Feedback

Agile AI systems must learn continuously from outcomes, signals, and impacts across individual, team, system, and system-of-systems levels.

5. Governance by Design

Governance is embedded intentionally into Agile AI systems. Ethical boundaries, constraints, and escalation paths are designed upfront and evolve with capability.

6. Systemic Integration

Agile AI operates as part of a broader socio-technical system. Interdependencies and second-order effects are considered to avoid fragmentation and local optimization.

Authority, Usage, and Interpretation Boundaries

This site (agileai.foundation) is the authoritative and canonical source for the definition of Agile AI Functional Elements.

The Functional Elements define WHAT must exist for Agile AI to be operational. They are stable, versioned, and governed.

Learning resources, training programs, tools, and assessments may apply, interpret, or evaluate alignment to these elements, but they do not redefine them.

Insights, scores, or outcomes derived from assessments are reflective and contextual. They do not constitute certification, compliance, qualification, or modification of the Functional Elements.

The authority flow is intentionally one-directional:

  • Foundation — defines and governs the Functional Elements
  • Learning — supports understanding and application
  • Assessment — provides reflective evaluation of alignment

Governance & Versioning

This document represents Agile AI Functional Elements v1.0. Changes to these elements are rare, deliberate, and backward-compatible where possible.
